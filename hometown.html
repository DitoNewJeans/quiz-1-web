    Hometown

    Jakarta

    Jakarta, the capital city of Indonesia, is a bustling metropolis located on the northwest coast of Java, the most populous island in Indonesia. With a population exceeding 10 million people, Jakarta is not only the largest city in Indonesia but also one of the most populous urban areas in Southeast Asia. The city is known for its vibrant and diverse culture, with people from various ethnic backgrounds, religions, and traditions coexisting harmoniously. Jakarta serves as the political, economic, and cultural heart of Indonesia. It's a major economic hub with a diverse economy, including finance, trade, manufacturing, and services. The city is home to numerous multinational corporations, government institutions, and a thriving startup scene. Jakarta's cultural scene is equally dynamic, featuring a mix of traditional and contemporary arts, music, and cuisine. While the city faces challenges related to traffic congestion and flooding, it continues to grow and evolve as a dynamic and influential global city in Southeast Asia.
